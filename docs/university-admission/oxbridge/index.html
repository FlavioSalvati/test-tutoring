The application process for Oxbridge, which comprises the universities of Oxford and Cambridge, is distinct from that of most other UK universities. To apply for Oxbridge, students must first choose either Oxford or Cambridge and research the various courses and colleges available at each university to select the one that aligns with their interests and career aspirations.After choosing the university and course, the next step is to craft a compelling personal statement. The statement should be well-written, tailored to the course being applied for, and demonstrate a passion for the subject while highlighting academic accomplishments, skills, and experience. Following this, applicants must apply through UCAS by the mid-October deadline, which is a few weeks earlier than most other universities.Students may also need to sit for one or more admissions tests, such as the TSA, LNAT, STEP, MAT, PAT, TMUA, NSAA, ENGAA, HAT, or ELAT, depending on the course they are applying for. These tests are intended to assess academic potential and aptitude for the course, and usually take place in October or November, apart rom the STEP which takes place in June.If candidates meet the academic requirements, they will be invited to attend at least two interviews, which usually take place in December. The interviews will be subject-specific and will include questions related to the course selected, as well as general questions about interests and experiences. Results of the application are then released in January.

A tutor can help you with your Oxbridge application by providing guidance on the following key areas:Personal Statement: A tutor can provide you with feedback on your personal statement, ensuring that it is well-written, tailored to the course, and showcases your passion for the subject.Admissions Test: Depending on the course you are applying for, you may be required to take an admissions test. A tutor can help you prepare for the test by providing by teaching you the knowledge and exam technique required to do well on it.Interview: The interview is an essential part of the Oxbridge application process. A tutor can help you prepare for the interview by conducting mock interviews, providing Oxbridge interview questions and feedback on your performance. They can also give you advice on how to answer common interview questions and help you develop your communication and interpersonal skills.

Getting accepted to Oxford and Cambridge is highly competitive. According to the UCAS statistics for the 2020 admissions cycle, there were over 40,000 applications for around 6,000 places across both universities. This makes the acceptance rate just under 15%. However, the acceptance rates vary depending on the course and college. For instance, the acceptance rate for Medicine is around 10%, and for Law is approximately 14%. Conversely, courses such as Philosophy and Theology have higher acceptance rates of about 21%.For the 2020 admissions cycle, the University of Oxford received 23,020 applications, with 11,230 of those applications coming from the UK, and 11,790 coming from international applicants. Similarly, the University of Cambridge received 21,885 applications, with 10,435 applications coming from the UK, and 11,450 applications from international students.

The requirements for admission to Oxbridge universities, comprising the universities of Oxford and Cambridge, are stringent and highly competitive. Most courses at both universities require strong Oxbridge grades of A*AA or higher, sometimes with these grades to be in specific subjects. Additionally, most courses require strong GCSE grades, with an expectation of at least eight subjects graded 7, 8, or 9 (A or A*).It&#39;s important to note that the specific requirements differ depending on the course and college you are applying to. For example, courses in mathematics and the sciences typically have higher entry requirements than other courses, with many science courses requiring at least two A*s.Most students applying to Oxbridge universities take four Oxbridge subjects, although offers usually only require three Oxbridges. The average Oxbridge student takes at least nine GCSEs, although some courses may require or prefer more. For international students, there is also an English language requirement, for example getting the required grades in the TOEFL or IELTS exams.
