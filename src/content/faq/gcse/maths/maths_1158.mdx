The conversion factor between metres and millimetres is defined as follows: 

$$
1 \text{ metre} = 1,000 \text{ millimetres}
$$

To grasp this conversion, it is essential to recognize that the metric system operates on a base of ten. This structure simplifies the process of converting between various measurement units. Specifically, the prefix "milli-" denotes one-thousandth, which means that one millimetre (mm) is equal to one-thousandth of a metre (m).

When converting from metres to millimetres, the procedure involves multiplying the number of metres by $1,000$. For instance, if you have $2$ metres and wish to determine how many millimetres that represents, you would perform the following calculation:

$$
2 \text{ metres} \times 1,000 = 2,000 \text{ millimetres}
$$

This result arises from the fact that each metre contains $1,000$ millimetres; thus, you must multiply by $1,000$ to convert to millimetres.

On the other hand, if you have a measurement in millimetres and wish to convert it back to metres, you would divide by $1,000$. For example, if you have $5,000$ millimetres, the conversion to metres would be:

$$
5,000 \text{ millimetres} \div 1,000 = 5 \text{ metres}
$$

This operation effectively reverses the initial process, allowing you to translate millimetres back into the larger units of metres.

Understanding these conversions is vital across various fields, including science, engineering, and everyday applications. It enables you to accurately measure and compare lengths, whether you are engaged in a school project, constructing an object, or simply aiming to enhance your comprehension of distances.
