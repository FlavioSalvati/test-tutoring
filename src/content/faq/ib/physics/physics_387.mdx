Power is fundamentally connected to energy consumption over time, as it represents the rate at which energy is utilized or transferred.

To elaborate, power is a physical quantity that quantifies the amount of energy supplied by a source or absorbed by a load within a specified time interval. It is measured in watts ($W$), which is equivalent to joules per second ($J/s$). Therefore, if a device has a power rating of $1 \, W$, it consumes $1 \, J$ of energy every second.

The relationship among power, energy, and time can be succinctly expressed by the equation:

$$
P = \frac{E}{t}
$$

In this equation, $P$ denotes power, $E$ represents energy, and $t$ signifies time. This formula illustrates that power is directly proportional to energy and inversely proportional to time. In simpler terms, the greater the amount of energy consumed or the shorter the time taken to consume that energy, the higher the power output.

If both bulbs are illuminated for the same duration, the $100 \, W$ bulb will consume more energy due to its higher power rating. Conversely, if both bulbs are to consume the same amount of energy, the $60 \, W$ bulb must remain on for a longer period.

This relationship between power, energy, and time is crucial in various fields of physics and engineering. It plays a significant role in the design and operation of electrical and electronic systems, where power efficiency is a vital consideration. Understanding this relationship enables us to make informed decisions about energy use in our daily lives, from selecting energy-efficient appliances to minimizing our carbon footprint.
