---
title: "Why might firms with market power resist innovation and disruptors?"
summary: "Firms with market power might resist innovation and disruptors to maintain their dominance and avoid potential risks."

Firms that possess market power often exhibit a reluctance to embrace innovation and disruptive changes, primarily to preserve their dominance and mitigate potential risks.

To elaborate, companies with a substantial market share are typically inclined to maintain the status quo. This inclination stems from their considerable investments in existing business models, technologies, and strategies. Any significant alterations could jeopardize these investments. For example, a firm that has dedicated years to developing and refining a specific technology may hesitate to adopt a new, disruptive technology that could render its current innovations obsolete.

Additionally, innovation inherently carries a degree of risk. New technologies or business models may not achieve the anticipated levels of success or profitability, with the possibility of outright failure always looming. Firms with market power, which tend to be larger and more established, are often particularly risk-averse because they have more at stake. Consequently, they may prefer to rely on their proven methods rather than venture into untested ideas.

Another factor contributing to the resistance of market-power firms towards innovation and disruptors is the fear of losing their competitive edge. If a firm offers a distinctive product or service that differentiates it from competitors, it may be cautious about changes that could dilute this uniqueness. For instance, a firm holding a monopoly on a particular technology might be hesitant to adopt a new technology that could easily be replicated by rival companies.

Lastly, regulatory barriers can also hinder innovation within firms that have significant market power. In numerous industries, emerging technologies or business models often encounter substantial regulatory challenges, which can be both costly and time-consuming to navigate. As a result, established firms may prefer to continue utilizing their existing technologies and business models, which are already aligned with regulatory requirements.

In summary, while innovation and disruption can yield tremendous benefits, they also present risks and challenges. Firms with market power, owing to their size, established positions, and vested interests, are likely to exhibit greater resistance to such changes.
