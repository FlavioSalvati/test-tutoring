---
title: "How do mergers and acquisitions increase market power?"
summary: "Mergers and acquisitions increase market power by reducing competition and increasing the firm's control over the market."
author: "Dr. Michael Turner"

---

Mergers and acquisitions (M&A) can significantly enhance market power by diminishing competition and increasing a firm's control over the market.

When two companies merge or one company acquires another, the total number of competitors in the market decreases. This reduction in competition can result in a greater market power for the combined entity. Market power refers to a firm's ability to influence the price of its products or services. With fewer competitors, a firm has more latitude to set its prices, as customers have limited alternatives.

Additionally, mergers and acquisitions can boost a firm's market power by expanding its market share. By merging with or acquiring another company, a firm typically gains access to that company's customer base. This can substantially increase the firm's market share, giving it greater influence over market pricing decisions.

Furthermore, M&A can lead to economies of scale, which can further enhance a firm's market power. Economies of scale occur when a firm's average costs decrease as it increases production. When companies merge or one company acquires another, they can often pool their resources and streamline operations, resulting in more efficient production of goods or services. This increased efficiency can lower costs, allowing the firm to underprice its competitors and thus strengthen its market power.

Moreover, mergers and acquisitions can enhance a firm's bargaining power with suppliers and customers. A larger firm typically has more leverage in negotiations, enabling it to secure more favorable terms. This advantage can reduce the firm's costs and increase its profitability, further bolstering its market power.

It is essential to recognize that while mergers and acquisitions can amplify a firm's market power, they may also attract regulatory scrutiny. Competition authorities frequently examine proposed M&A transactions to ensure they do not lead to excessive market power that could harm consumers. If a merger or acquisition is deemed likely to significantly reduce competition, it may be blocked or subjected to conditions intended to mitigate its impact on the market.
