Radiation plays a vital role in medical imaging, allowing healthcare professionals to visualize the internal structures of the body.

Medical imaging serves as an essential tool for diagnosing and treating a wide variety of medical conditions. Several imaging techniques utilize radiation, including X-rays, computed tomography (CT) scans, and nuclear medicine scans.

X-rays employ a minimal amount of radiation to produce images of bones and other dense tissues within the body. In contrast, CT scans utilize a series of X-ray images to generate detailed three-dimensional representations of the body. These imaging modalities are particularly effective for diagnosing bone fractures, pulmonary issues, and other conditions that impact tissue density.

Although radiation is a crucial asset in medical imaging, it can be harmful if not administered correctly. Medical professionals exercise great caution to ensure that patients receive the lowest feasible dose of radiation during imaging procedures. They also carefully evaluate the advantages of imaging against the potential risks associated with radiation exposure.
